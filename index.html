A example blog post with image

By Inspirononline , Posted May 5, 2017 , In Articles

The word “change” brings a lot of different emotions in different people. For some it could be daunting, for others it could be sad, but for me it’s exciting. Change can vary from a major change in one’s love or life or career, or a subtle change in the way one spends his leisure. Change, be it major or subtle, can make a huge difference in one’s life. So now, what all can bring changes in one’s lives? Well, one can say a vital decision, marriage, child-birth, death of a loved one, break-up from a romantic relationship, losing a job, moving to a new place, falling in love and what not? There was a crucial change in me and the way I looked at life because of none of these reasons but because of a dream! A dream, not a cherished aspiration, but a series of thoughts, images, and sensations occurring in one’s mind during sleep. A dream that I had during a nap, changed my perspective and i started seeing life under a new light with renewed energy. I do not remember even a bit of this dream but just the after-effects of it remained with me since I woke up. I had no reason, yet the smile on my lips did not vanish. I had to hurry up to catch up with the new found energy, what if it just evaporated, I could not afford to lose it, for it was creating such emotions that I had felt never before. An invisible spell was cast on me, I was enchanted. It did not ask me to do anything or be anyone, it just showed me how to be happy, how to walk on the surface of things without being affected by them, a very important life lesson, how to live in the moment. After embracing it, water tasted better and air smelled good. I still stayed in the same house, I still went to the same office, I still met the same people I had always met, I still had the same friends, hobbies, interests, likes and dislikes, actually everything was still the same, yet everything and everyone looked different. The difference was not the same as seeing the glass as half empty or as half full, it was beyond comparison, it was a profound experience like being born again. I became a new person, new in numerous ways but not sapient enough to preach my learnings to others. All that I can and I do is that, I wish everyone has a dream, be it whether in the form of a person on an experience or whatever that I had, so that one is born again and rises above all the problems and difficulties that one faces in one’s lives.

BEING HAPPY: IS IT REALLY WHAT IT SEEMS TO BE?

By Inspirononline , Posted April 24, 2017 , In Articles

A BMW zooms in and screeches to a halt. A handsome and well-dressed guy hops out and grins. A stunningly beautiful lady runs up and embraces him. Close shot of the guy’s face – He’s smiling from ear to ear. Seems utterly happy. Right? I just described modern pop culture’s (a significant portion anyway) definition of happiness that is being peddled to countless susceptible individuals. Many of us strive to achieve material and sexual comforts and unable to do so, fall into the well of unhappiness. A few of us do manage to achieve these goals, but how many can truly say – “Yes. I couldn’t be happier!”? So what exactly is happening? Too many people I meet on a daily basis think landing their dream job, owning a home, getting married and having kids, or even making that promotion they’ve been dying for, will surely lead to elevated levels of happiness. Yet, when it comes to the cut and they receive a new joining date or get that marriage proposal, they find reality to be quite different. Happiness seems to depend on external and internal factors, both equally necessary. While external factors are largely achievable through dedication and hard work, internal factors become a bit indefinite and hazy. But they can be achieved with hard work too!

CHILDHOOD AND ADOLESCENT DEPRESSION

By Inspirononline , Posted April 11, 2017 , In Articles

Often, we find ourselves looking enviously at children and their free spirited laughter and innocence and think, “Just look at them! Not a care in the world, not a single bill to pay”. If you’re thinking this, the woes of adult-ing have hit you, my friend. Walking down memory lane, most of us may find that our childhood was innocent and carefree. Even if school was a bother, we had friends, cousins and neighbours to cheer us up and aid our enjoyment. It was like a school after-party. That’s most children. Some children, although, have a more difficult time dealing with their childhood. Just like adults with depression, some children also may be susceptible to episodes of depression. The portrayal of these of depression in children may come off as anxiety and social withdrawal. They may be afraid to be left alone or have difficulty making friends. They also find expressing themselves emotionally quite challenging. There may be several reasons behind this. Children with undetected learning disabilities, those who may be bullied or picked on by a peer or adult, children who may have experienced physical or sexual abuse, or children who have poor or maladaptive social exposure and as a result may have difficult times in social scenarios may increase the chances of children suffering from depression.
